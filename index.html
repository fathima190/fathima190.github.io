02 / Teaching

Teaching as a way
of thinking.

Teaching, for me, is an extension of how I learn. I built a small learning community around neuroimaging and EEG because actively teaching and explaining forces me to retrieve, test, and deepen my own understanding. The students benefit, and so do I. It is the most honest kind of learning I know.

01
History and Principles FirstBreaking down the physics and clinical context before diving into data, so nothing feels arbitrary or disconnected.
02
Active Recall MethodsStudents retrieve, apply and defend their understanding, moving far beyond passive note-taking. This is how the community consolidates knowledge together.
03
Clinical and Cognitive ContextConnecting imaging modalities to real-world applications in research and patient care, from psychiatric conditions to healthy cognition.

03 / UX Research

Design rooted in
human understanding.

My UX work is grounded in the same rigour I bring to cognitive research: understanding people, their mental models, and what actually helps them.

01 / Research Methods
User Research

Qualitative and quantitative methods including interviews, usability testing, and behavioural analysis, to surface what users truly need rather than what they say they want.

02 / Synthesis
Cognitive Lens

Bringing a cognitive neuropsychology background to UX means thinking carefully about attention, memory load, and decision-making in interface design. My thesis on stimulus-driven internal attention directly informs this.

03 / Application
Health & Research Tools

Particularly interested in UX for research software, clinical tools, and health applications, where usability has direct human consequence.
