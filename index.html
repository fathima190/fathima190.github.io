headshot here

I study how the brain pays attention — using eye-tracking, EEG, and neuroimaging to understand how people perceive and interact with the world. My work sits at the intersection of cognitive neuroscience and human-computer interaction, with a focus on designing accessible, community-centred assistive technologies for people with disabilities.

My Areas of Expertise

Psychophysiological Methods

EEG, eye-tracking, ECG/HRV, fMRI — end-to-end from data collection to analysis pipelines.

Cognitive Neuroscience

Attention, working memory, covert spatial attention, microsaccade analysis, predictive processing.

Assistive & Gaze-Based Interfaces

Designing and evaluating accessible technologies — gaze-based and brain-computer interfaces — for diverse and resource-limited contexts.

Data Analysis

Python (MNE, pandas, scipy), MATLAB, SPM — custom analysis scripts and statistical modelling of neuroimaging and biosignal data.

I Think I'm Good At

Translating messy psychophysiological data into clean, meaningful insights — and connecting rigorous lab research to real-world accessibility impact.
